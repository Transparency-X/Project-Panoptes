# Introduction

Surveillance cults are a modern evolution of high-control groups that use systematic monitoring, coercive control, and psychological manipulation to dominate their members. While physical isolation was historically the primary tool of cult leaders, modern groups employ pervasive surveillance—both physical and digital—to track activities, enforce compliance, and prevent dissent.

A "surveillance cult" combines traditional cult dynamics (charismatic leadership, thought reform, isolation) with omnipresent monitoring. By tracking location, communications, finances, and even forcing the revelation of private thoughts, leadership maintains absolute power over their followers.

This documentation accompanies the structured dataset in `data/` and provides narrative context.
