# The Four Pillars of Cult Surveillance

Not all surveillance is the same. High-control groups generally utilize four distinct mechanisms to maintain the illusion of omniscience:

1. **Technological / Digital Surveillance**  
   The use of modern technology to track members 24/7. This includes mandated location-sharing apps (e.g., Life360), forced installation of spyware/screen-sharing software on personal devices, demanding passwords, and monitoring bank accounts.

2. **Peer-to-Peer Surveillance (Weaponized Informing)**  
   Cultivating a "Panopticon" environment where members are required to report on one another. Friendships, marriages, and family bonds are weaponized. If a member witnesses a rule violation and fails to report it, they face equal punishment.

3. **Institutional Espionage**  
   Advanced groups maintain dedicated intelligence wings. These factions employ private investigators, aggressive litigation, wiretaps, and physical harassment to monitor and intimidate current members, whistleblowers, and critics.

4. **Self-Surveillance (Internalized Control)**  
   The ultimate goal of a surveillance cult. Through frequent mandatory confessions, severe punishments, or the threat of releasing blackmail, members become too terrified to even *think* dissenting thoughts, believing the leadership can see their minds.
